Introduction

From: Brighton, UK
For Fans Of: Glassjaw, Thrice, At The Drive-In, Deftones

Since the band's inception, Windowhead's aim has been to create its own unique musical vision by incorporating different textures and approaches from a wide variety of musical styles. This goal was initially realised in 2018's Still Life, which combined the huge crescendos of Post-Rock with the hazy acoustic guitars of Lo-Fi Indie.

On Sunflowers (2021), the band developed its sound by incorporating influences from Post-Hardcore, Shoegaze and Metal. The album is defined by the juxtaposition of a dreamlike atmosphere of swirling, ethereal guitars and layered vocals with the sinister edge provided by thundering drums and bass. 

Windowhead has spent the last 3 years honing its craft as a live band, leading to an increased focus on immediacy inherited from Post-Hardcore influences such as Thrice, Glassjaw and At The Drive-In. The impact of this can be felt in the upcoming  Terrestrials EP, which combines the band's signature effects-heavy guitars with unconventional time signatures, extreme dynamics and chaotic rhythms.

Terrestrials

Every aspect of Terrestrials is characterised by Windowhead's development as a live band. Unpredictable shifts in dynamics between ethereal soundscapes and crushing climaxes are designed to grab an audience's attention by catching it off guard. Meanwhile, tracks such as Cat Insulin and Todd, Honey  feature hooks that will bury themselves deep into the listener's mind.

Windowhead's newfound focus on reaching out to their audience is also reflected in Terrestrials' lyrical content. Previously, the band's work has been highly metaphorical, allowing the listener to build their own interpretation. This EP takes a more direct approach, focusing on topics such as toxic masculinity, environmental collapse and the role of the media in reinforcing the status quo.

The EP's name comes from its first track, Ex-Terrestrial. This song uses the contrast between low, heavy riffing and a soaring hook to highlight the divide between ordinary people and the billionaires who hope to escape the consequences of their actions on Earth by becoming "ex-terrestrials" and colonising other planets. But instead of making this the title of the EP, Windowhead has chosen to name it after those who would be left behind; recognising the importance that Terrestrials must place upon community if they are to build a better future.
